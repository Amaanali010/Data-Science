# 📊 Inferential Statistics (Easy Explanation)

## 1. Definition

**Inferential Statistics** is used to **make predictions or guesses about a large group (population) using a small group (sample).**

---

## 2. In Simple Words

👉 We **do not study everyone**
👉 We study **a few people**
👉 Then we **guess about everyone**

---

## 3. Important Terms

* **Population** = Whole group
  (Example: All students in a school)

* **Sample** = Small part of the group
  (Example: 50 students from that school)

---

## 4. Easy Example

### 🎓 School Example

* Total students = **1000** (Population)
* Students surveyed = **100** (Sample)

Result:

```id="ex1"
70 out of 100 students like math
```

👉 Conclusion (Inference):
**About 70% of all students may like math**

---

## 5. Another Example

### 🍔 Food Example

You taste **one spoon** of soup 🍲

👉 If it tastes good, you say:
**“The whole soup is good”**

This is **Inferential Statistics**
(because you judged everything using a small part)

---

## 6. Why We Use It

* Saves **time** ⏱️
* Saves **money** 💰
* Easy to study small data

---

## 7. Key Points

* Uses **sample data**
* Makes **predictions**
* Talks about the **whole population**
* Results are **not always 100% exact**

---

## 8. Simple Summary

👉 Study a **small group**
👉 Make a **guess about a big group**

✅ That is **Inferential Statistics**
