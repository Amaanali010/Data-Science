# 🌿 Lecture: What is Classification?

## 📌 1. What is Classification?

Classification means **grouping things based on their similarities and differences**.

👉 **In simple words:**  
It is a way to **organize living things into groups so they are easier to study**.

**Examples:**
- We group books in a library by subject  
- We group clothes by type (shirts, pants, etc.)

Similarly, scientists group living organisms.

---

## 📌 2. Why Do We Need Classification?

Classification helps us to:

- Understand the huge variety of living organisms  
- Study them in a systematic way  
- Identify organisms easily  
- Understand relationships between different organisms  

---

## 📌 3. Types of Classification

### 🔹 Artificial Classification
- Based on simple and visible features  
- Example: grouping plants by size (small, medium, large)  

👉 **Problem:** Not always accurate  

---

### 🔹 Natural Classification
- Based on many characteristics (structure, function, etc.)  
- Shows natural relationships  

👉 **More scientific than artificial classification**  

---

### 🔹 Phylogenetic Classification
- Based on evolutionary relationships  
- Shows how organisms are related through history  

👉 **Most advanced and widely used today**  

---

## 📌 4. Levels of Classification (Hierarchy)

Living organisms are classified into different levels:

1. Kingdom  
2. Phylum  
3. Class  
4. Order  
5. Family  
6. Genus  
7. Species  

👉 **Easy trick to remember:**  
**King Philip Came Over For Good Soup**

---

## 📌 5. Example

Let’s classify a human:

- Kingdom: Animalia  
- Phylum: Chordata  
- Class: Mammalia  
- Order: Primates  
- Family: Hominidae  
- Genus: Homo  
- Species: sapiens  

👉 **Scientific name:** *Homo sapiens*

---

## 📌 6. Key Points to Remember

- Classification = grouping based on similarities  
- Helps in easy study and understanding  
- There are different types (artificial, natural, phylogenetic)  
- Organisms are arranged in levels (hierarchy)  

---
